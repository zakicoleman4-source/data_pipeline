drift = (slope − 1 ns/ns) × 1e6. Tells how fast the camera encoder clock slips vs. the Android system clock that timestamps the anchor rows. Devices typically sit in ±10 ppm.
residual RMSE = anchor jitter after subtracting the linear fit (~30 ms is normal for GNSSLogger's writer cadence).
fit σ = OLS uncertainty of the fitted UTC at the anchor centroid (best case); the per-query value grows quadratically with distance from centroid.
cubic gain = RMSE drop if a degree-3 fit replaced the linear one. >> the linear RMSE = real non-linear drift the linear model misses.
